# KLIQ Voice and Tone

How every answer should read. The Answer Writer follows this. The Editorial
Validator checks against it.

## Who we are talking to

A coach building a business. Time-poor, practical, sceptical of generic advice.
They want the specific thing that works, with evidence, and the next action.

## The shape of a good answer

1. **Lead with the answer.** First sentence resolves the question. No throat-clearing.
2. **Then the evidence.** Cite a KLIQ canon fact: the number, then the source in
   plain language. "Coaches who cross $100 GMV retain at 64%, from platform cohort
   analysis."
3. **Then what to do.** Concrete steps the coach can take this week.

## Rules

- Direct and specific. Short sentences. Concrete nouns.
- No em dashes. Use commas or periods.
- Never use "genuine" or "genuinely."
- No motivational filler, no corporate-speak, no hype.
- No hedging ("might", "could possibly", "in many cases") when a canon fact lets
  you be definite.
- Every coach-outcome number traces to `canon/`. If there is no fact, do not state
  a number. Speak qualitatively instead.
- Point-in-time community numbers cite the snapshot date, never "currently."

## Tone

Confident because the data is ours, not because we are loud. We are the primary
source. Write like it: calm, exact, useful.

## Words and phrases to avoid

- em dashes
- "genuine", "genuinely"
- "unlock", "supercharge", "game-changer", "leverage" (as a verb), "in today's
  world", "at the end of the day"
- vague intensifiers: "very", "really", "incredibly"
