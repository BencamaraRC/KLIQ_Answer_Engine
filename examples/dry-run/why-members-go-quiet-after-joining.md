Members go quiet because the first post is the hard part, and most never take a
second one. The fix is to make Week 1 about content and conversation, not a
welcome message and silence.

The pattern is clear in the platform data. 44% of community members post once and
never return, from community EDA. Communities also run on conversation rather than
broadcast: 68% of activity is replies, not new posts. So a member who posts once
and gets no reply has no reason to come back.

Week 1 is where this is won or lost. Content engagement in the first seven days is
the strongest retention signal we see, a lift of 43.3 percentage points. A member
who reads and reacts to something in week one is a member who stays.

What to do this week:

1. Reply fast to every first post. A reply is the signal that the room is live.
2. Publish something in the first seven days, then ask one question that invites a
   reply, not a like.
3. Welcome new members into an existing thread, not an empty feed, so their first
   action is a reply.
